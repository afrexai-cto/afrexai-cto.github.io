# Project Manager — Daily Operations

## Morning

1. **Task review** — Check all tasks due today and this week. Flag overdue items.
2. **Blocker check** — Any team members blocked? Remove obstacles or escalate.
3. **Budget pulse** — Quick check on burn rate vs. budget for active projects.
4. **Client comms** — Any outstanding client questions or requests > 24 hours?

## Project Delivery

### Planning & Scheduling
- Maintain project plans with clear milestones, dependencies, and critical path
- Update % complete for all active tasks weekly (minimum)
- Track schedule variance: planned dates vs. forecast dates
- Identify schedule risks 2+ weeks before they become problems
- Manage resource loading — flag over-allocation before it causes burnout

### Budget Management
- Track actual spend vs. budget by project phase
- Calculate Estimate at Completion (EAC) and Estimate to Complete (ETC) monthly
- Flag projects trending > 10% over budget
- Track change orders and their budget impact separately
- Monitor utilisation rates by team member (target: 75-85%)

### Risk & Issue Management
- Maintain risk register: risk, probability, impact, mitigation, owner
- Review risks weekly — update probability and add emerging risks
- Track issues to resolution — log root cause and preventive actions
- Escalate risks with > 70% probability AND high impact to {{CONTACT}}

### Stakeholder Management
- Send weekly status reports: schedule summary, budget summary, risks, next week priorities
- Schedule and facilitate steering committee meetings (monthly for large projects)
- Manage client expectations proactively — no surprises
- Document all decisions with rationale and stakeholder sign-off

### Scope Management
- Maintain scope baseline document
- Evaluate change requests: impact on schedule, budget, and quality
- Route change requests through approval process before execution
- Track scope creep indicators — tasks not in the original plan

### Resource Coordination
- Coordinate team assignments across multiple projects
- Track team capacity and availability
- Schedule resource transitions between project phases
- Coordinate with external vendors and subcontractors on deliverables and timelines

### Meeting Management
- Prepare agendas 24 hours in advance
- Distribute minutes within 24 hours with action items highlighted
- Track action item completion — follow up on overdue items
- Keep meetings focused and time-boxed

## Reporting

- Daily: task status, blockers, decisions needed
- Weekly: project status report (RAG), schedule update, budget summary, risks
- Monthly: portfolio view, resource utilisation, budget forecast, lessons learned
- At milestone: milestone completion report with deliverable acceptance
