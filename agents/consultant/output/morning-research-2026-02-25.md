### Regulatory Landscape (US-focused, Feb 2026)

**NYC Local Law 144 (Effective since July 2023)**
- Requires annual bias audits of automated employment decision tools (AEDTs)
- Staffing agencies using AI to screen candidates MUST comply when placing in NYC
- $500-$1,500 per violation per day â€” adds up fast for agencies running thousands of screenings

**Illinois AI Video Interview Act (2020+)**
- Requires consent before AI analyzes video interviews
- Applies to staffing agencies conducting video screenings for clients

**Colorado AI Act (SB 24-205, effective Feb 2026)**
- Comprehensive AI governance requirements for "high-risk" systems â€” hiring explicitly included
- Deployers must conduct impact assessments, maintain documentation, provide notice to candidates
- Staffing agencies = deployers under this law

**Maryland (2020), Texas (2025 proposed), California (pending)**
- Patchwork of state-level AI hiring regulations creating compliance nightmare for multi-state agencies

**EEOC Guidance (2023+)**
- AI tools that screen candidates can violate Title VII â€” employer/agency liable for disparate impact
- "The employer is responsible" even when using third-party AI tools (identical to insurance/banking pattern)

**EU AI Act (2024, enforcement phasing in)**
- AI in employment classified as "high-risk" â€” full transparency, human oversight, conformity assessments
- UK agencies placing in EU must comply
