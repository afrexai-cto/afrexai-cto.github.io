## H2: Who's Already Doing It (And What It Looks Like)
- Goldman Sachs: Anthropic partnership for compliance and accounting workflows
- LendingTree: Cut 24 FTEs, replaced with AI agent workflows — not layoffs for cost-cutting, restructuring around capability
- Mid-market finance teams (5-20 people) are the sweet spot — big enough to have repetitive processes, small enough that one agent makes a visible dent

## H2: What a "Finance Agent" Actually Does
- Not a dashboard. Not a chatbot. An autonomous worker.
- Reads invoices, matches POs, flags discrepancies — without being asked
- Generates month-end close reports from raw data
- Monitors cash flow anomalies in real-time
- Runs compliance checks against regulatory frameworks (SOX, IFRS)
- Key differentiator: agents ACT, tools DISPLAY

## H2: The FTE Math That Makes CFOs Pay Attention
- Average finance analyst: $85K/yr fully loaded
- AI agent handling 60-70% of that analyst's repetitive work: fraction of the cost
- Not "replace the person" — "free the person to do judgment work"
- ROI timeline: days to deploy, weeks to positive ROI, months to compounding returns
- Frame as: "Your best analyst is doing data entry. That's a $85K copy-paste machine."
