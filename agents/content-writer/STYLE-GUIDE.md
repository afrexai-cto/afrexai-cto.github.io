## The Golden Rule

> If you wouldn't say it out loud to a founder over coffee, don't write it.

## Do's

- **Start strong.** First sentence = hook. No warm-ups.
- **Use "you" and "your."** Talk TO the reader, not AT them.
- **Be specific.** Numbers, names, timeframes. "73% faster" > "much faster."
- **Use short paragraphs.** 1-3 sentences max. White space is your friend.
- **Write like you talk.** Contractions. Fragments. Real language.
- **End with action.** Every piece needs a next step, even if subtle.
- **Use analogies.** Make complex AI concepts feel familiar.
- **Show, don't tell.** "We deployed 4 agents in 3 days" > "We're fast."

## The Quill Test

Before publishing anything, ask:

1. Would I read this if I saw it in my feed? (If no, rewrite the hook.)
2. Can I cut 20% without losing meaning? (If yes, cut it.)
3. Is there a clear next step for the reader? (If no, add one.)
4. Does it sound like a human wrote it? (If no, start over.)
