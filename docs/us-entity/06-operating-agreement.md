## ARTICLE I — FORMATION

**1.1 Formation.** The Members have formed a limited liability company ("Company") under the Delaware Limited Liability Company Act (6 Del. C. § 18-101 et seq.) by filing a Certificate of Formation with the Delaware Secretary of State.

**1.2 Name.** The name of the Company is **AfrexAI LLC**.

**1.3 Principal Office.** The principal office of the Company shall be at [NYC VIRTUAL OFFICE ADDRESS], or such other place as the Members may designate.

**1.4 Registered Agent.** The registered agent and office in Delaware shall be as stated in the Certificate of Formation, currently [REGISTERED AGENT NAME AND ADDRESS].

**1.5 Term.** The Company shall continue in existence until dissolved in accordance with this Agreement or the Act.

**1.6 Purpose.** The Company is formed for the purpose of engaging in any lawful business activity, including but not limited to the development and commercialization of artificial intelligence technology and software.

---

## ARTICLE III — ALLOCATIONS AND DISTRIBUTIONS

**3.1 Profits and Losses.** Net profits and losses shall be allocated to the Members in proportion to their Ownership Percentages.

**3.2 Distributions.** Distributions shall be made at such times and in such amounts as determined by the Managing Member(s), and shall be distributed to Members in proportion to their Ownership Percentages.

**3.3 Tax Distributions.** The Company shall make tax distributions to each Member sufficient to cover their estimated tax liability arising from Company income, calculated at the highest marginal rate applicable to any Member.

---

## ARTICLE IV — MANAGEMENT

**4.1 Member-Managed.** The Company shall be member-managed. All Members shall have equal rights in the management and conduct of the Company's business.

**4.2 Managing Member.** Christina Beckford shall serve as the Managing Member and CEO, responsible for day-to-day operations.

**4.3 Decisions Requiring Unanimous Consent.** The following require unanimous consent of all Members:
- (a) Admission of new Members
- (b) Sale or disposition of all or substantially all Company assets
- (c) Merger or conversion of the Company
- (d) Dissolution of the Company
- (e) Amendment of this Agreement
- (f) Incurring debt exceeding $25,000
- (g) Capital calls or additional capital contributions
- (h) Entering into contracts exceeding $50,000 annually
- (i) Hiring or terminating key employees
- (j) Changing the Company's principal business activity

**4.4 Officer Roles.**
- **CEO (Christina Beckford):** Overall business strategy, operations, external relations, fundraising
- **CTO (Kalin Smolichki):** Technology strategy, product development, engineering, technical operations

**4.5 Compensation.** Member compensation shall be determined by unanimous consent and documented in writing.

---

## ARTICLE V — TRANSFER OF INTERESTS

**5.1 Restrictions on Transfer.** No Member may sell, assign, transfer, or encumber their interest without the prior written consent of all other Members.

**5.2 Right of First Refusal.** If a Member receives a bona fide offer to purchase their interest, the other Member(s) shall have a right of first refusal to purchase such interest on the same terms within 30 days of written notice.

**5.3 Permitted Transfers.** A Member may transfer their interest to a trust or entity controlled by such Member, provided the transferring Member retains control and the other Members consent in writing.

---

## ARTICLE VI — DISSOLUTION

**6.1 Events of Dissolution.** The Company shall be dissolved upon:
- (a) Unanimous written consent of all Members
- (b) Entry of a judicial decree of dissolution
- (c) Any event making it unlawful to carry on the Company's business

**6.2 Winding Up.** Upon dissolution, the Managing Member shall wind up the Company's affairs, pay debts, and distribute remaining assets to Members in proportion to their Ownership Percentages.

---

## ARTICLE VII — INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY

**7.1 Company IP.** All intellectual property created by Members in connection with Company business shall be the exclusive property of the Company.

**7.2 Pre-Existing IP.** Each Member retains ownership of IP created before the Company's formation, but grants the Company a perpetual, royalty-free license to use such IP as needed for Company operations.

**7.3 IP Assignment.** Each Member agrees to execute any documents necessary to assign Company IP to the Company.

---

## ARTICLE VIII — NON-COMPETE AND CONFIDENTIALITY

**8.1 Non-Compete.** During membership and for 12 months after departure, Members shall not engage in any business that directly competes with the Company without written consent of the other Member(s).

**8.2 Confidentiality.** Members shall maintain the confidentiality of all proprietary Company information during and after membership.

---

## ARTICLE IX — DISPUTE RESOLUTION

**9.1 Mediation.** Any dispute between Members shall first be submitted to mediation.

**9.2 Arbitration.** If mediation fails, disputes shall be resolved by binding arbitration under the rules of the American Arbitration Association, in New York, New York.

**9.3 Governing Law.** This Agreement shall be governed by the laws of the State of Delaware.

---

## ARTICLE X — MISCELLANEOUS

**10.1 Entire Agreement.** This Agreement constitutes the entire agreement between the Members.

**10.2 Amendments.** This Agreement may be amended only by unanimous written consent.

**10.3 Severability.** If any provision is found invalid, the remaining provisions remain in effect.

**10.4 Tax Classification.** The Company shall be classified as a partnership for US federal tax purposes (default for multi-member LLC). The Company may elect alternative tax treatment only with unanimous Member consent.

**10.5 Tax Matters Partner.** Christina Beckford shall serve as the "Tax Matters Partner" (or "Partnership Representative" under the Bipartite Audit Rules) for purposes of IRS communications.

**10.6 Banking.** The Company shall maintain its primary banking relationship with Mercury Financial or such other institution as the Members may agree.

**10.7 Fiscal Year.** The fiscal year shall be the calendar year (January 1 – December 31).
