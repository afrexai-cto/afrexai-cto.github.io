# Acceptable Use Policy

**AfrexAI, Inc.**
**Version:** 1.0 | **Effective Date:** [DATE] | **Owner:** [Security Lead Name] | **Next Review:** [DATE + 1 year]

---

## 1. Purpose

This policy defines the acceptable use of AfrexAI's information systems, devices, networks, and data by all personnel. It protects AfrexAI, its employees, and its customers from harm caused by misuse of technology resources.

## 2. Scope

All AfrexAI founders, employees, contractors, and anyone with access to AfrexAI systems or data. Covers all company-owned and personal devices used for company business (BYOD).

## 3. General Use

- AfrexAI systems and data are provided for business purposes. Limited personal use is permitted provided it does not interfere with work, consume excessive resources, or violate this policy.
- Users are responsible for the security of their accounts and must not share credentials.
- All activity on AfrexAI systems may be logged and monitored for security purposes.

## 4. Acceptable Use

Personnel **may:**
- Use company systems for authorized business activities
- Access customer data only as required to perform their job function
- Use approved software and SaaS tools
- Store business data in approved, encrypted locations
- Use company email and messaging for professional communication

## 5. Prohibited Use

Personnel **must not:**

### 5.1 Security
- Share passwords, API keys, or access credentials with anyone (including other team members — use a password manager's sharing feature if needed)
- Disable security controls (MFA, disk encryption, firewall, screen lock)
- Install unauthorized software on devices used for company business
- Connect to production systems from public, unsecured networks without VPN
- Store customer data on personal devices or unapproved cloud services
- Bypass access controls or attempt to access systems beyond their authorization

### 5.2 Data
- Access customer data without a legitimate business need
- Copy customer data to local devices, personal accounts, or unapproved locations
- Share customer data with unauthorized parties
- Use customer data for any purpose other than delivering contracted services
- Send Confidential or Restricted data via unencrypted channels

### 5.3 AI Systems
- Modify AI agent behavior, prompts, or configurations without following change management procedures
- Use customer data to train, fine-tune, or evaluate AI models without explicit customer consent
- Disable AI agent logging or audit trails
- Test AI agents against production customer data without authorization

### 5.4 General Conduct
- Use company systems for illegal activities
- Harass, threaten, or discriminate against anyone via company systems
- Misrepresent their identity or AfrexAI in communications
- Use company resources for personal commercial ventures
- Intentionally introduce malware or malicious code

## 6. Device Security (Including BYOD)

Any device used to access AfrexAI systems must meet these requirements:

- [ ] Full disk encryption enabled
- [ ] Screen lock set to ≤5 minutes
- [ ] Operating system and applications kept up to date
- [ ] Anti-malware / EDR installed (when required by Information Security Policy)
- [ ] Password/biometric authentication enabled
- [ ] Remote wipe capability enabled (or agreement to wipe company data on departure)

## 7. Email & Communications

- Do not open suspicious attachments or click unverified links — report phishing to the Security Lead
- Do not auto-forward company email to personal accounts
- Use professional judgment in all company communications
- Be cautious with AI-generated phishing, which may appear highly convincing

## 8. Internet Use

- Do not download pirated software or copyrighted material
- Do not visit sites known to distribute malware
- Use VPN when working from public networks
- Browser extensions must be minimized; do not install extensions that request broad permissions

## 9. Reporting Violations

- Report any suspected policy violations or security concerns to the Security Lead immediately
- Reports may be made without fear of retaliation
- Failure to report known violations is itself a violation of this policy

## 10. Enforcement

- Violations are investigated by the Security Lead
- Consequences range from verbal warning to termination, depending on severity and intent
- Intentional violations involving customer data or system security may result in immediate termination and legal action
