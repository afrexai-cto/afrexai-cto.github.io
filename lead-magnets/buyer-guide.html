02 What to Look for in an AI Agent Provider

Not all AI agent services are equal. Here's what separates serious providers from vaporware:

Must-Have Capabilities

  • Multi-model architecture ‚Äî providers locked into a single LLM (GPT-only, Claude-only) create vendor risk. Best providers use the right model for each task.
  • Human-in-the-loop escalation ‚Äî agents must know when they're uncertain and escalate to humans. Ask to see their escalation logic.
  • Custom workflow building ‚Äî your business is unique. Cookie-cutter agents don't work. The provider should build agents around YOUR processes.
  • Observability dashboard ‚Äî you should see every action an agent takes, in real time. Full audit trail, not a black box.
  • Iterative improvement ‚Äî agents should get better over time. Ask about their feedback loop and retraining process.

Nice-to-Have Differentiators

  • Dedicated success manager (not just a support ticket queue)
  • Industry-specific templates and training data
  • Integration marketplace (pre-built connectors for common tools)
  • SLA-backed performance guarantees
  • Month-to-month contracts (confidence signal)
‚úÖ Pro tip: Ask for a live demo with YOUR data (anonymized if needed), not a polished sales demo with fake data. How they handle this request tells you everything.

03 Red Flags That Should Kill a Deal

We've analyzed dozens of failed AI agent deployments. These patterns predict failure:

üö© Immediate Deal-Breakers
  • "Our AI replaces your entire team" ‚Äî No serious provider says this. Agents augment humans; they don't eliminate them (yet).
  • No audit trail or logging ‚Äî If you can't see what the agent did, you can't trust it. Period.
  • Requires admin access to all systems ‚Äî Agents should work with minimum necessary permissions. Over-provisioning is a security red flag.
  • 12+ month contract minimum with no pilot ‚Äî Confident providers offer trials. Long lock-ins hide bad product.
  • "Our proprietary AI" with no model transparency ‚Äî Most providers use foundation models (OpenAI, Anthropic, Google). If they won't say which, ask why.
  • No customer references in your industry ‚Äî They may be experimenting on you. Get references or walk.
  • Vague pricing tied to "usage" ‚Äî Unpredictable bills are the #1 complaint. Get flat-rate or capped pricing.
üü° Yellow Flags (Proceed with Caution)
  • Only case studies, no live demo
  • Can't articulate their escalation policy
  • Sales team can't answer technical questions without "getting back to you"
  • No SOC 2 or equivalent security certification (in progress = okay; not planned = red)
  • Team size under 10 with enterprise pricing

05 Security & Compliance Checklist

Use this checklist during vendor evaluation. Every item should be addressed before signing.

  • SOC 2 Type II certification (or Type I with Type II in progress)
  • Data encryption at rest (AES-256) and in transit (TLS 1.3)
  • Data residency options (US, EU, or your required regions)
  • No training on your data without explicit opt-in consent
  • Role-based access control for agent service accounts
  • Complete audit trail for all agent actions (exportable)
  • Incident response plan with defined SLAs (< 4hr for critical)
  • Data retention and deletion policies (right to delete)
  • Subprocessor list (which LLM providers, cloud infrastructure)
  • Penetration testing (annual minimum, results shareable under NDA)
  • Business continuity / disaster recovery plan
  • GDPR, CCPA, HIPAA compliance (as applicable to your industry)
  • Insurance coverage (cyber liability, errors & omissions)
  • Secure development lifecycle (SDLC) documentation
  • Background checks for employees with access to customer data
Important: If a vendor says "we use OpenAI/Anthropic so their security covers us" ‚Äî that's not how it works. The vendor is a data processor; they need their OWN security certifications for how they handle your data between you and the model provider.

08 Questions to Ask During Sales Calls

Copy these into your next vendor meeting:

Technical Questions

  1. Which foundation models do you use, and how do you select models per task?
  2. Walk me through your agent's decision tree for a task it can't complete.
  3. How do you handle agent errors? Show me a real example.
  4. What's your average accuracy rate at 30, 60, and 90 days?
  5. Can I export all agent activity logs?

Business Questions

  1. What's your average time to value (first measurable ROI)?
  2. Can I talk to 3 customers in my industry?
  3. What happens if I need to cancel after month 2?
  4. How do you handle scope changes mid-engagement?
  5. What's included in the monthly fee vs. what costs extra?

Security Questions

  1. Where is my data stored, and who has access?
  2. Do you train your models on customer data?
  3. Can I see your SOC 2 report and pen test summary?
  4. What's your incident response SLA?
  5. How do you handle subprocessor changes?
