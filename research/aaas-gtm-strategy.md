- **Deliverability:** Warm domains for 2 weeks before sending; keep under 50/day per inbox

### LinkedIn Specifics

- **Content:** 3–5 posts/week showing agent results, behind-the-scenes, ROI stories
- **DM approach:** Comment on their posts first → connect → value-first DM → soft CTA
- **Founder-led selling:** The CEO's personal brand is the #1 asset at this stage

## 2. Sales Cycle Length

### By Company Size

| Segment | Employees | Deal Size | Typical Cycle | Notes |
|---------|-----------|-----------|---------------|-------|
| **SMB** | 10–100 | $1,500–$3,000/mo | **2–4 weeks** | Often single decision maker (owner/CEO); fast yes or no |
| **Mid-Market** | 100–1,000 | $3,000–$8,000/mo | **4–8 weeks** | 2–3 stakeholders; may need pilot/POC |
| **Enterprise** | 1,000+ | $8,000–$12,000+/mo | **3–6 months** | Procurement, legal, security review; multi-stakeholder |

### By Vertical

| Vertical | Cycle Modifier | Why |
|----------|---------------|-----|
| **Real Estate** | Shorter (−2 weeks) | Entrepreneurial buyers; less bureaucracy; ROI is obvious (leads, follow-up) |
| **Insurance** | Average | Mid-size agencies decide fast; carriers are slow |
| **Legal** | Longer (+2 weeks) | Risk-averse; need to address confidentiality thoroughly |
| **Healthcare** | Longer (+4 weeks) | HIPAA compliance review adds time; committee decisions |
| **Financial Services** | Longest (+4–8 weeks) | Heavy compliance (SOC 2, regulatory); multiple approval layers |

### Acceleration Tactics
1. **Free pilot / POC** — Offer 2-week free pilot of one agent. Reduces perceived risk dramatically. Conversion from pilot to paid: typically 50–70% for good products.
2. **ROI calculator** — Show dollar savings before the call. "You're spending $6,500/mo on [task]. Our agent costs $2,500/mo."
3. **Urgency framing** — "We onboard 3 new clients per month to ensure quality. Next slot is [date]."
4. **Champion enablement** — Give your internal champion a one-pager to sell internally. They do half the work for you.
5. **Remove procurement friction** — Monthly contracts (not annual) for first 3 months. Accept credit card. No 40-page MSAs.

### Primary Buyers (Who Signs the Contract)

| Vertical | Primary Buyer | Title | What They Care About |
|----------|--------------|-------|---------------------|
| **Real Estate** | Brokerage Owner / Managing Director | CEO, Managing Partner | Revenue growth, agent productivity, lead conversion |
| **Insurance** | Agency Principal or VP Operations | CEO, COO, VP Ops | Policy processing speed, customer retention, operational costs |

| **Healthcare** | Practice Administrator or VP Operations | COO, VP Ops, Practice Manager | Patient throughput, admin burden reduction, compliance |
| **Financial Services** | Head of Operations or CTO | COO, CTO, VP Technology | Compliance automation, reporting speed, cost reduction |

### Buying Committee (Who Influences)

Typical 3–5 person committee at mid-market:

| Role | Influence | What They Evaluate |
|------|-----------|-------------------|
| **Economic Buyer** (CEO/COO) | Final approval; budget authority | ROI, strategic fit, risk |
| **Technical Evaluator** (CTO/IT Director) | Veto power on tech | Security, integration, data handling |
| **End User** (Operations Manager) | Champions or kills adoption | Ease of use, workflow fit, daily impact |
| **Legal/Compliance** | Veto power on risk | Data privacy, liability, regulatory compliance |
| **Finance** (CFO/Controller) | Budget gate | Cost vs. headcount savings, contract terms |

### Selling to the Committee
- **Never** sell to just one person. Map the committee early ("Who else would be involved in a decision like this?")
- Give each stakeholder what they need: ROI deck for CEO, security whitepaper for CTO, workflow demo for ops manager
- The **Operations Manager** is often your best champion — they feel the pain daily
- The **CTO/IT Director** is the most common blocker — address security/integration proactively

## 4. Common Objections & Responses

### Objection 1: "We can build this ourselves"

**Reality:** 90% of companies that say this never ship. Those that do spend 6–12 months and $150K+ in engineering time.

**Response framework:**
> "Absolutely, and some companies do. The question is whether AI agent development is your core competency or a distraction from it. Our clients typically find that by the time they've hired, built, and maintained an internal system, they've spent 3–5x what our managed service costs — and they're still 6 months behind where they could have been. We can have your first agent running in [X] days."

**Proof points:** Calculate their build cost: 1 senior engineer ($180K/yr) + 6 months + infrastructure + ongoing maintenance = $150K+ first year vs. $18K–$36K/year with AfrexAI.

### Objection 2: "I'm not sure AI is ready / I don't trust AI"

**Response framework:**
> "Healthy scepticism is smart. That's actually why managed AI works — you're not betting on raw technology; you're getting a team that monitors, corrects, and improves the agents continuously. Our agents handle [specific process] with [X]% accuracy, and a human reviews anything flagged. Would a 2-week pilot with zero commitment help you evaluate?"

**Key:** Offer a pilot. Let the results speak. Sceptics who see their own data processed correctly become the strongest advocates.

### Objection 3: "What about data security / compliance?"

**Response framework:**
> "We take this seriously — here's our security overview [hand them the document]. We use [encryption standards], data never leaves [jurisdiction], we're working toward SOC 2 [or have it], and we sign BAAs for healthcare clients. We're happy to do a security review with your IT team before any data touches our system."

**Action items for AfrexAI:**
- Create a 2-page security overview document
- Implement SOC 2 Type I (or at minimum, document your security practices formally)
- Prepare a BAA template for healthcare clients
- Get DPA (Data Processing Agreement) ready for all clients
- Be ready for a security questionnaire (create a pre-filled CAIQ or SIG)

### Objection 4: "It's not in the budget"

**Response framework:**
> "I understand budget is tight. Let me ask — what's the current cost of [the process the agent replaces]? Most of our clients find the agent pays for itself within 30 days when you factor in [salary savings / time savings / error reduction / revenue increase]. For example, [Client X] was spending $8,000/month on [task] and replaced it with a $3,000/month agent — saving $60K/year."

**Key:** Always reframe from cost to ROI. The agent isn't an expense; it's a cheaper, faster employee.

### Objection 5: "We need to think about it" / stalling

**Response framework:**
> "Of course. To help with your evaluation, can I send over a quick ROI analysis specific to your business? And what would need to be true for this to be a clear yes?"

**Then:** Follow up in 3, 7, and 14 days. If no response after 3 touches, move to nurture sequence.

### Objection 6: "What happens if we want to leave?"

> "Your data is yours. We export everything in standard formats with 30 days' notice. No lock-in — we earn your business monthly."

## 5. Case Study Analysis — Companies Selling Managed AI/Automation Services

### 1. **Relevance AI** (Australia → US expansion)
- **What they sell:** AI workforce platform — managed AI agents for sales, support, operations
- **Playbook:** Product-led growth + outbound. Free tier to get users in, then upsell managed services. Heavy LinkedIn content from founders. Raised $15M+.
- **Lesson for AfrexAI:** Founder-led content on LinkedIn drives massive awareness at zero cost. Their CEO posts daily.

### 2. **Lindy.ai**
- **What they sell:** AI employees (agents) that handle email, scheduling, research, sales
- **Playbook:** Viral product demos on Twitter/X, waitlist-driven launch, PLG motion, then enterprise sales team layered on top.
- **Lesson:** Short video demos of agents actually working (screen recordings) convert extremely well on social media.

### 3. **11x.ai**
- **What they sell:** AI SDRs (sales development reps) — "Alice" and "Mike" as AI workers
- **Pricing:** $5,000–$15,000/mo — similar range to AfrexAI
- **Playbook:** Outbound-first. Targeted enterprise sales. Named their agents like employees ("Alice handles your outreach"). Raised $50M+ Series B.
- **Lesson:** Giving agents human names and specific job titles makes the value proposition concrete. "Hire Alice, your AI SDR" > "Buy our AI platform."

### 4. **Thoughtful AI** (formerly Thoughtful Automation)
- **What they sell:** Managed AI agents specifically for healthcare revenue cycle management
- **Playbook:** Deep vertical focus (healthcare only). Speak the customer's language. Compliance-first messaging. Case studies showing exact dollar savings.
- **Lesson:** Vertical specialisation wins. They don't try to be everything — they're the "AI for healthcare billing" company.

### 5. **Capacity** (formerly Envision)
- **What they sell:** AI-powered support automation platform, managed service tier
- **Playbook:** Acquired smaller tools to expand capabilities. Channel partnerships with BPOs and MSPs. Enterprise sales with long-term contracts.
- **Lesson:** Channel partnerships with existing service providers (BPOs, MSPs) can scale faster than direct sales.

### 6. **Automation Anywhere** (Managed RPA → AI Agents)
- **What they sell:** Evolved from RPA to AI agent platform with managed services
- **Playbook:** Partner ecosystem (500+ partners). Co-selling with Deloitte, Accenture, etc. Enterprise-first. Industry-specific solutions.
- **Lesson:** At scale, the partner channel can drive 40–60% of revenue.

### 7. **UiPath** (Managed Automation)
- **What they sell:** Automation platform with managed service options
- **Playbook:** Community-led growth (free tier + training academy). Then enterprise sales. Strong partner ecosystem.
- **Lesson:** Free training/certification creates evangelists who then bring you into their companies.

### 8. **Smith.ai**
- **What they sell:** AI + human receptionist and intake services for law firms and SMBs
- **Pricing:** $200–$2,000/mo — managed service model
- **Playbook:** Hyper-focused on legal vertical initially. SEO + content marketing (blog ranks for "virtual receptionist for law firms"). Partnerships with legal tech companies (Clio, etc.).
- **Lesson:** Own one vertical completely before expanding. Their legal content strategy drives consistent inbound leads.

### 9. **Botpress / Voiceflow** (Managed Chatbot Services)
- **What they sell:** Conversational AI platforms with agency/managed service layers
- **Playbook:** Build platform → attract agencies → agencies sell managed services to end clients. Two-sided ecosystem.
- **Lesson:** Agencies and consultancies can be your salesforce if you make it easy for them to resell.

### 10. **Wizard AI** (AI for Insurance)
- **What they sell:** AI agents for insurance claims processing and underwriting
- **Playbook:** Deep vertical focus on insurance. Compliance-first. White-glove onboarding. Named AI agents for specific tasks.
- **Lesson:** Insurance buyers need hand-holding. White-glove onboarding and dedicated success managers close deals.

### Common Patterns Across Winners
1. **Vertical focus** — The most successful start with 1–2 verticals, not "AI for everyone"
2. **Founder-led content** — CEO on LinkedIn/Twitter showing the product, sharing insights
3. **Named agents with job titles** — "Hire Sarah, your AI paralegal" not "Buy our AI platform"
4. **Free pilot / POC** — Reduce friction; let the product sell itself
5. **Outbound + content hybrid** — Outbound for immediate pipeline, content for long-term inbound
6. **Compliance-first messaging** — Especially in regulated industries (healthcare, finance, legal)
7. **Channel partnerships** — MSPs and consultancies as force multipliers

## 6. Channel Partnerships

### High-Value Partner Types

#### A. Managed Service Providers (MSPs)
- **Who:** IT companies managing tech stacks for SMBs and mid-market
- **Why:** They already have trusted relationships and monthly retainers with your target customers
- **Model:** White-label or referral. MSP adds AI agents to their service stack, marks up 20–30%.
- **Target MSPs:** Those serving legal, healthcare, financial services specifically
- **How to find:** Search "managed IT services for law firms" / "healthcare IT support" on Google; attend Channel Partners Conference

#### B. Business Process Outsourcing (BPO) Firms
- **Who:** Companies that handle back-office operations (data entry, customer support, document processing)
- **Why:** AI agents directly enhance/replace what they sell. They can position AI agents as premium tier.
- **Model:** OEM or white-label. BPO integrates your agents into their service delivery.

#### C. Management Consultancies (Boutique)
- **Who:** Small/mid-size consultancies advising on operations, digital transformation
- **Why:** They advise on the problems your agents solve. Natural referral source.
- **Model:** Referral fee (10–15% of first-year revenue) or co-selling
- **How to find:** LinkedIn search "operations consultant" + your target verticals

#### D. Vertical Software Vendors
- **Who:** CRM/practice management software for specific industries (Clio for legal, Salesforce for real estate, Epic integrators for healthcare)
- **Why:** Integration partnerships create stickiness and access to their customer base
- **Model:** Integration + co-marketing. App marketplace listing.

#### E. Accounting / Bookkeeping Firms
- **Who:** CPAs and bookkeeping firms serving SMBs
- **Why:** They see their clients' financial pain and can recommend efficiency tools
- **Model:** Referral partnership with commission

### Partnership Activation Playbook
1. Identify 10 potential partners per category
2. Reach out via LinkedIn or warm intro (not cold email for partners)
3. Offer: "We'll give you 2 free agents for your own business + 15% recurring revenue share on referrals"
4. Create a partner one-pager and demo video they can share
5. Joint webinar within 60 days of partnership
6. Quarterly business reviews to keep the partnership active

## 8. First 10 Customers Playbook

### Phase 1: Foundation (Week 1–2)

**Goal:** Get your sales infrastructure ready.

- [ ] **Define your ICP precisely:** Pick ONE primary vertical to start (recommend: Legal or Real Estate — fastest sales cycles, clearest ROI, least compliance friction)
- [ ] **Name your agents:** Give each agent a job title and human name. "Emma, AI Paralegal" not "Document Processing Agent"
- [ ] **Create sales assets:**
  - 1-page overview PDF
  - 5-slide pitch deck
  - 2-minute demo video (screen recording of an agent working)
  - ROI calculator (even a simple spreadsheet works)
  - Security overview (1–2 pages)
- [ ] **Set up outbound infrastructure:**
  - 3 email domains warmed up (2 weeks to warm)
  - Apollo.io or Clay for contact enrichment
  - Instantly or Smartlead for sequences
  - Calendly for booking
- [ ] **Prepare pilot programme:** 2-week free pilot, clearly scoped, with success metrics defined upfront

### Phase 2: Mine Your Network (Week 2–4)

**Goal:** Get customers 1–3 from warm connections.

- [ ] **CRM blitz:** Go through all 368 companies. Tag the top 50 most likely buyers. Prioritise by: (a) in target vertical, (b) you have a relationship, (c) right company size
- [ ] **Personal outreach to top 20:** Direct email or LinkedIn DM from the CEO. Not a sales pitch — a conversation: "We've built something I think could save your team 20+ hours/week. Can I show you in 15 minutes?"
- [ ] **Ask for referrals:** Email everyone in your network: "We're launching managed AI agents for [vertical]. Know anyone who might benefit? Happy to give them a free pilot."
- [ ] **Founder's LinkedIn push:** Post daily about what you're building. Show the agents working. Share the journey.
- [ ] **Target:** 3 pilots started by end of week 4

### Phase 3: Outbound Engine (Week 3–8)

**Goal:** Get customers 4–7 from cold outreach.

- [ ] **Build target list:** 500 companies in your primary vertical, enriched with decision-maker contacts
- [ ] **Launch cold email sequences:** 50–75 emails/day across 3 domains
- [ ] **Sequence structure:**
  - Email 1: Pain point + social proof ("We helped [Company X] save 120 hours/month...")
  - Email 2: Case study or short video (3 days later)
  - Email 3: Direct ask with calendar link (5 days later)
  - Email 4: Breakup email (7 days later — "Should I close your file?")
- [ ] **LinkedIn parallel track:** Connect with same contacts; comment on their posts; DM after engagement
- [ ] **Follow-up relentlessly:** 80% of deals close after the 5th touch. Most salespeople stop at 2.
- [ ] **Target:** 15–20 discovery calls booked, 5–7 pilots started

### Phase 4: Convert & Compound (Week 6–12)

**Goal:** Convert pilots to paid (customers 4–10), build referral engine.

- [ ] **Pilot success management:** Weekly check-ins during pilots. Share metrics. Show ROI.
- [ ] **Conversion conversation:** "Based on the pilot, your agent processed [X] tasks and saved [Y] hours. Here's what ongoing engagement looks like..." Monthly contract, no long-term commitment.
- [ ] **Case study from every convert:** Ask for a testimonial + permission to use their name/logo. Even a 2-sentence quote works.
- [ ] **Structured referral ask:** After conversion: "Who else in your network struggles with [same problem]? I'd love to offer them a pilot too. Happy to give you [incentive — month free, gift card, etc.]."
- [ ] **First webinar:** Host with your first 1–2 customers as guests. Their peers trust them more than they trust you.
- [ ] **Target:** 7–10 paying customers by week 12
