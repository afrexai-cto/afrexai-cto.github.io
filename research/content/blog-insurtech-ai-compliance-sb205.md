# AI Compliance for Insurtech: What Colorado SB 24-205 Means for Your Models

*By AfrexAI | February 2026*

---

If you're running AI underwriting models in insurance, you've probably heard about Colorado SB 24-205. But most insurtechs are underestimating what it actually requires — and the deadline pressure is real.

Here's the short version: Colorado now requires insurers using AI in coverage decisions to prove their models don't discriminate. Not "we think they're fair." Prove it. With documentation, testing, and ongoing monitoring.

## What SB 24-205 Actually Requires

The law targets "predictive models" used in insurance — underwriting, pricing, claims processing, marketing. If AI touches a consumer outcome, it's in scope.

**Three big obligations:**

1. **Governance framework.** You need documented policies for how AI models are developed, validated, and monitored. Not a Google Doc from 2023 — a living system.

2. **Bias testing.** Regular testing against protected classes (race, gender, age, disability). Not just at deployment — ongoing. Your model drift from Month 1 to Month 12 could create disparate impact you never intended.

3. **Transparency reporting.** The Colorado Division of Insurance can request your documentation at any time. If you can't produce it within 30 days, you've got a problem.

## Why This Matters Beyond Colorado

Colorado is the canary. Connecticut, Illinois, and New York are watching. The NAIC model bulletin on AI already covers similar ground. If you're only building for one state, you're building wrong.

The smart move: build a compliance framework that covers the strictest standard (Colorado) and maps to NAIC, EU AI Act, and emerging state laws. One framework, multiple jurisdictions.

## The Real Cost of Getting This Wrong

- **Regulatory action.** Fines, consent orders, forced model shutdowns.
- **Reputational damage.** "Insurtech caught discriminating" is a headline that kills your next funding round.
- **Operational chaos.** If a regulator orders you to stop using a model, what's your manual fallback? Most insurtechs don't have one.

## What a Compliance-Ready AI Stack Looks Like

**Model inventory.** Every AI model catalogued — what it does, what data it uses, who maintains it, when it was last validated.

**Automated bias detection.** Not a quarterly spreadsheet. Continuous monitoring that flags disparate impact as it emerges. You need to catch drift before a regulator does.

**Audit trail.** Every model decision logged with the inputs, outputs, and reasoning. When the Division of Insurance asks "why was this claim denied?", you need an answer in minutes, not months.

**Human oversight protocols.** Which decisions require human review? What's the escalation path? AI autonomy is fine until it isn't — you need clear guardrails.

## The 3-Week Path to Compliance

Most insurtechs think compliance is a 6-month project. It doesn't have to be.

**Week 1: Assessment.** Inventory your AI models, map data flows, identify gaps against SB 24-205 requirements.

**Week 2: Framework build.** Governance policies, bias testing protocols, monitoring dashboards. Not from scratch — from proven templates adapted to your stack.

**Week 3: Implementation.** Deploy monitoring agents, configure alerts, run initial bias tests, document everything.

After that, it's maintenance. Quarterly reviews, model revalidation, documentation updates. The hard part is the first three weeks.

SB 24-205 isn't going away. More states are coming. The insurtechs that build compliance into their AI stack now will have a structural advantage over the next 3-5 years. The ones that wait will be scrambling when New York or California passes their version.
