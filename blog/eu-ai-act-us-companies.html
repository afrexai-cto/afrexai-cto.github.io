Why US Companies Can't Ignore the EU AI Act Anymore

February 17, 2026 · 7 min read · AfrexAI

The EU AI Act isn't a European problem. It's your problem too.

If your company sells into the EU, has EU customers, or deploys AI systems that touch EU data — you're in scope. And the clock is ticking.

What Just Happened

February 2, 2026 marked the enforcement date for prohibited AI practices under the EU AI Act. Right now, today, certain uses of AI are flat-out illegal in the EU. Social scoring, manipulative AI, real-time biometric surveillance without judicial authorization — done.

But here's what most US companies are missing: the next wave hits August 2026. That's when high-risk AI obligations kick in. And "high-risk" covers a lot more than you'd think.

Are You High-Risk? (Probably.)

High-risk AI under the EU AI Act includes systems used for:

  • Employment decisions — hiring, performance evaluation, promotions
  • Credit scoring and lending — any AI that influences financial decisions
  • Insurance pricing — risk assessment, claims processing
  • Legal and judicial — evidence evaluation, legal research tools
  • Healthcare — clinical decision support, diagnostic AI, treatment recommendations

The Fines Are Real

Violation TypeMaximum Fine
Prohibited practices€35M or 7% of global annual turnover
High-risk violations€15M or 3% of global turnover
False info to regulators€7.5M or 1% of turnover

These aren't theoretical. The EU has proven with GDPR that they enforce. Meta paid €1.2B. Amazon paid €746M. They will come for AI violations too.

What You Need to Do Before August 2026

  1. AI System Inventory — Map every AI system. Classify each by risk level under the Act.
  2. Technical Documentation — Training data, design choices, performance metrics, known limitations.
  3. Human Oversight Protocols — Documented processes for human review of AI decisions.
  4. Conformity Assessment — Some high-risk systems require third-party audits before deployment.
  5. Bias and Fairness Testing — Regular testing with documented remediation plans.

The Overlap Problem

The EU AI Act doesn't exist in isolation. You also need to comply with GDPR, the EU Data Act, and sector-specific regulations (MiFID II, MDR, etc.). Three overlapping frameworks, all touching your AI systems.

Why this is an opportunity: EU-compliant = globally credible. The EU AI Act is becoming the global standard, just like GDPR did for privacy. First-mover advantage in regulated industries means you win every RFP where compliance matters.
