AI Agents for SaaS Companies

You're paying $85K/year engineers to copy data between systems. Your CS team spends more time on ticket triage than actual customer conversations. And your ops team? They're the glue holding together 47 different tools with Zapier duct tape and prayer.

AI agents replace the glue. Not the people.

Where SaaS Companies Bleed Time

Customer support triage and resolution. Agents handle Tier 1 tickets end-to-end. Password resets, billing questions, feature how-tos, integration troubleshooting — the stuff that makes up 60-70% of your ticket volume. They pull account context, check known issues, and resolve or escalate with full notes. Your CS team focuses on the complex stuff that actually needs a human.

User onboarding workflows. New signup comes in. Agent sends the welcome sequence, monitors product adoption milestones, triggers in-app guidance when usage stalls, and flags accounts showing churn signals at day 7. Not a static drip campaign — dynamic responses based on what the user actually does.

Revenue operations. Lead scoring, CRM hygiene, pipeline stage updates, deal velocity tracking. Agents keep Salesforce or HubSpot clean without anyone manually updating fields after every call. They catch deals stuck in stage for too long and ping the rep. They reconcile Stripe billing with CRM records nightly.

Internal reporting. MRR, churn, NRR, CAC, LTV — agents pull from Stripe, your database, Mixpanel, and wherever else your metrics live. Dashboard updates happen automatically. Board deck data is current by Friday morning without anyone spending half a day on it.

Bug report processing. Agents categorize incoming bug reports, check for duplicates against existing Jira or Linear tickets, enrich with user context and reproduction steps, and route to the right engineering team. Your PM stops playing traffic cop.

Reality Check

AI agents won't architect your product roadmap or have the empathy conversation with an enterprise customer threatening to churn. They won't debug a race condition in your payment flow. But they'll handle the operational machinery that eats 30-40% of your team's time — the repetitive, structured work that keeps scaling linearly with headcount when it shouldn't.
