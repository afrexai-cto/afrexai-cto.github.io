Step 1: Map Your Contract Workflow

Before automating anything, document your current process. Most organizations follow some version of this:

  1. Intake: Contract arrives via email, portal, or sales team
  2. Initial review: Someone reads it (or skims it) for red flags
  3. Clause extraction: Key terms, dates, obligations identified
  4. Comparison: Checked against company standards/playbook
  5. Redline: Changes marked and sent back
  6. Approval: Routed through internal approval chain
  7. Execution: Signed and filed

AI agents can automate steps 1-4 almost entirely, and significantly accelerate steps 5-6.

Your AI agent needs rules to follow. This is your contract playbook â€” the set of standards, acceptable ranges, and red flags specific to your business:

  • Standard terms: Payment terms (Net 30 acceptable, Net 90 flag), liability caps, indemnification language
  • Must-have clauses: IP assignment, confidentiality, termination rights, governing law
  • Red flags: Unlimited liability, auto-renewal without notice period, non-compete overreach, unilateral amendment rights
  • Approval thresholds: Contracts under $50K â†’ manager approval. Over $50K â†’ legal review. Over $250K â†’ C-suite sign-off.

Document this in a structured format. The more specific your playbook, the more accurately your AI agent performs. Vague rules produce vague results.

Step 3: Deploy the Contract Review Agent

A properly configured AI contract review agent handles the following automatically:

Intake & Classification

Contracts arriving via email are automatically detected, classified by type (NDA, MSA, SOW, vendor agreement, lease), and routed into the review queue. No manual sorting.

Clause Extraction

The agent reads the full contract and extracts every key clause: payment terms, liability limits, IP provisions, termination conditions, renewal terms, SLAs, and more. Results are presented in a structured summary â€” not buried in a 40-page PDF.

Playbook Comparison

Each extracted clause is compared against your playbook. The agent flags deviations, scores risk level (low/medium/high/critical), and generates a deviation report with specific page and section references.

Auto-Approval or Escalation

Contracts that fall within all acceptable ranges get auto-approved and routed for signature. Contracts with deviations get escalated to the appropriate reviewer with a pre-built summary of exactly what needs attention â€” and suggested redline language.

Step 4: Build the Obligation Tracker

Contract review doesn't end at signature. The most expensive contract failures happen post-execution â€” missed deadlines, forgotten renewal dates, unfulfilled obligations.

Your AI agent should automatically:

  • Extract all dates, deadlines, and milestones from signed contracts
  • Create calendar entries and reminders for renewal dates (30, 60, 90 days out)
  • Track performance obligations and delivery milestones
  • Alert responsible parties when deadlines approach
  • Flag contracts approaching auto-renewal for review/renegotiation
A single missed auto-renewal on an unfavorable vendor contract can cost more than a year of AI agent deployment. The obligation tracker pays for itself on day one.

Step 5: Measure and Optimize

Track these metrics monthly to validate ROI:

  • Review time: Average days from intake to approval (target: <2 days for standard contracts)
  • Clause accuracy: Percentage of clauses correctly extracted and classified
  • Escalation rate: What percentage need human review? (Lower = better playbook)
  • Missed obligations: Should trend to zero within 90 days
  • Cost per contract: Total review cost divided by contracts processed

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Starting too broad. Don't try to automate every contract type on day one. Start with your highest-volume, most standardized contracts (NDAs, vendor agreements) and expand from there.
  • Vague playbooks. "Reasonable payment terms" isn't actionable. "Net 30 acceptable, Net 45 requires manager approval, Net 60+ requires legal review" is.
  • Skipping the obligation tracker. Extraction without monitoring is half a solution. The biggest value is preventing post-signature failures.
  • Zero human oversight. AI agents handle the volume â€” humans handle the judgment calls. Build escalation paths, not full autonomy.
